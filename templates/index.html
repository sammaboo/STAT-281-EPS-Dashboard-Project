EPS Predictions & Forecasting
Story Point
Forecast future quarterly EPS using six methods: linear regression, exponential smoothing, moving average, analyst-adjusted estimates, Holt-Winters (triple exponential smoothing with seasonality), or SARIMA (seasonal ARIMA). The surprise pattern chart shows historical beat/miss trends. Surprise analysis breaks down seasonality by quarter and tracks the rolling average over time. Adjust the time frame to focus predictions on recent data. Confidence scores are computed via leave-last-4-out backtesting - the model is trained on all data except the last 4 quarters, then its predictions are compared to actual values. The resulting MAPE (Mean Absolute % Error) is converted into a confidence score.

Post-Earnings Announcement Drift
Story Point
One of the most well-documented anomalies in finance: stocks that beat earnings estimates tend to keep rising in the following quarter, while stocks that miss tend to keep falling. The left chart groups all earnings events into surprise quintiles and shows the average next-quarter return for each group. The right chart shows the raw scatter.
