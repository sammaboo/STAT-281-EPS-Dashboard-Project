Analyst Dispersion
Story Point
This graphs displays analyst disagreement. This is calculated by taking the standard deviation of individual EPS estimates divided by the absolute consensus value. Higher dispersion means less agreement among analysts. Spikes in dispersion often precede earnings surprises or periods of uncertainty.

Post-Earnings Announcement Drift
Post-Earnings Announcement Drift
Story Point
This scatterplot tests the Post Earnings Announcment Drift hypothesis. Each dot is one earnings event. The OLS trendline shows the relationship between surprise % and next-quarter return. In most modern research, larger positive earnings surprises produce larger positive stock returns. The large percentage of datapoints in the top right quadrant support this idea.
